Research Philosophy

Building AI That Serves Humanity

The most exciting AI breakthroughs mean nothing if they can't be deployed responsibly. My work focuses on developing models that are not only state-of-the-art in performance but also aligned with human values, transparent in their reasoning, and robust against misuse. Every system I build starts with the question: who does this help, and how do we make sure it stays that way?

Bridging Research and Application

Too much brilliant research never leaves the lab. I specialize in taking novel architectures, training techniques, and safety methods from academic papers and turning them into production-ready systems. Whether it's fine-tuning foundation models for specific domains or designing evaluation frameworks that catch failure modes before deployment, the goal is always the same — make the research real.

Open Knowledge, Shared Progress

AI progress accelerates when knowledge flows freely. I actively contribute to open-source projects, publish research openly, and speak at conferences to share findings with the broader community. Collaboration isn't just a value — it's a strategy for building AI that works for everyone, not just the organizations with the largest compute budgets.
